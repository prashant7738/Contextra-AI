**Core Concepts:**\n\n* Multiprocessor system: an interconnection of two or more CPUs with memory and input-output equipment.\n* Synchronization: needed to enforce the correct sequence of processes and ensure mutually exclusive access to shared writable data.\n* Multiprocessor systems: classified as MIMD (multiple instruction stream, multiple data stream) systems.\n* Inter-processor communication: facilitated through a common memory or input-output channels.\n* Multiprocessing: improves system reliability and performance.\n\n**Must Remember:**\n\n* Synchronization is crucial in multiprocessor systems to prevent data corruption and ensure correct process execution.\n* Multiprocessor systems can be classified as MIMD systems, supporting concurrent operations.\n* Inter-processor communication is essential for data exchange and synchronization.\n* Multiprocessing improves system reliability and performance.\n* There are three operating system organizations for multiprocessors: master-slave, separate, and distributed.\n\n**Quick Revision Checklist:**\n\n1.
